## Design Philosophy — "Things I Notice"
- **Users do not read.** They scan, guess, and move fast. The UI has to still work.
- **Most bugs are unclear decisions.** The design usually skipped a rule, a state, or an edge case.
- **Edge cases are the product.** The happy path is easy. Real life is not.
- **Speed beats correctness.** People pick the fastest path, even if it is not the intended one.
- **If it needs explaining, it needs redesigning.** Clarity is cheaper than training.
- **Systems break quietly first.** I look for early cracks before they become support tickets.
- **Motion is feedback, not decoration.** If it does not clarify state, I cut it.
- **Constraints are where the design is.** Technical, legal, and business limits shape the real solution.

## How I Work
1. **Start with what breaks** — I map failure states, edge cases, and recovery paths before visuals.
2. **Find where people hesitate** — I look for moments of confusion, drop-off, and misclicks.
3. **Make the rules explicit** — I define the system, states, and logic so it holds up at scale.
4. **Prototype to kill bad ideas early** — I test behavior and flow before polishing UI.
5. **Ship, then refine** — I iterate based on data, feedback, and what actually happens in the wild.
